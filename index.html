The Humble Administrator's Garden

The Humble Administrator's Garden, located at No.178 Dongbei Street in Suzhou, Jiangsu Province, China, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the four most famous gardens in China.

Originally built in 1509 during the Ming Dynasty, the garden was designed by the famous painter Wen Zhengming and was named by its owner, Wang Xiancheng, who likened himself to a humble administrator tending to his garden rather than government affairs.

Ticket price: 70-90 yuan

Open time: 07:30-17:30

The Lingering Garden

The Lingering Garden, also known as Liuyuan Garden, is a renowned classical private garden situated in Suzhou, China. Located at No. 338 Liuyuan Road, outside Changmen Gate in Suzhou.

Historically, the garden changed hands multiple times and was renamed the Hanbi Villa by its second owner, Liu Shu, during the Qing Dynasty's Jiaqing reign. It was later renovated and renamed the Lingering Garden by Sheng Xuanhuai and his father at the end of the Tongzhi reign. The garden is particularly known for its exquisite space design of the buildings, with halls that are spacious yet elegant.

Ticket price: 45-55 yuan

Open time: 07:30-17:30 for 1 Mar.-31 Oct. 07:30-17:00 for 1 Nov.-28/29 Feb.

The Master of the Nets Garden

The Master of the Nets Garden, located in the southeast corner of Suzhou's old city within the Kuojiatou Alley by Fengmen, was originally built during the Southern Song Dynasty and was known as "Yuyin."

It was purchased and rebuilt around 1770 during the Qianlong period of the Qing Dynasty by the retired minor official of the Board of Rites, Song Zongyuan, and renamed “Master of the Nets Garden.” This garden is famous for its exquisite layout, profound cultural connotations, and elegant atmosphere.

Ticket price: 30-40 yuan

Open time: 07:30-17:30 for 16 Apr.-30 Oct. 07:30-17:00 for 31 Oct.-15 Apr.

Suzhou Gardens

Poetic Jiangnan water town gardens, the "Mother of Eastern Gardens"

No.1
The Humble Administrator's Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, centered around water, elegant and generous, it is one of the representative gardens of Jiangnan.

No.2
The Lingering Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, renowned for its exquisite architectural art, with grand and magnificent halls and diverse courtyards, offering the pleasure of mountains and forests without leaving the city.

No.3
The Master of the Nets Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, a water garden themed around the spirit of fishing, with a compact layout and a delicate style.

No.4
The Lion Grove Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, famous for its rockeries, with winding caves, strange rocks, and a style reminiscent of the Yuan Dynasty.

No.5
The Huanxiu Mountain Villa

National 5A tourist attraction, known for its rockeries, with an ingenious layout, full of charm, where mountains have veins and waters have sources.

No.6
The Jingsi Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, featuring grand and spacious halls, elegant and exquisite, drawing from the past and innovating for the future.

No.7
The Canglang Pavilion

National 5A tourist attraction, the oldest with natural scenery, simple and elegant architecture, a typical urban mountain forest.

No.8
The Yiyu Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, with a simple and sparse style, natural and smooth, an important example for the study of garden history.

No.9
The Couple's Garden

National 5A tourist attraction, with an exquisite central residential area, and an eastern garden known for its yellow stone rockeries and exquisite wood carvings.
